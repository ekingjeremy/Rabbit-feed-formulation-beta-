# CATTLE BREEDS IN NIGERIA - Complete Guide

## INDIGENOUS BEEF CATTLE BREEDS

### 1. WHITE FULANI (BUNAJI)
**Origin:** West Africa (Most common in Nigeria)
**Classification:** Zebu (Bos indicus)

**Characteristics:**
- Coat color: White to light gray
- Body type: Medium to large frame
- Horns: Long, lyre-shaped
- Hump: Well-developed
- Weight: Bulls 300-500 kg, Cows 250-350 kg

**Nutritional Requirements:**
**Growing (6-18 months):**
- CP: 12-14%
- TDN: 65-70%
- ME: 2550-2650 kcal/kg
- Ca: 0.4-0.6%
- P: 0.3-0.4%

**Maintenance (Adult):**
- CP: 9-11%
- TDN: 55-60%
- ME: 2400-2500 kcal/kg
- Ca: 0.3-0.4%
- P: 0.2-0.3%

**Lactating Cows:**
- CP: 12-14%
- TDN: 65-70%
- ME: 2600-2700 kcal/kg
- Ca: 0.6-0.8%
- P: 0.4-0.5%

**Production Data:**
- Daily weight gain: 350-500 g
- Mature weight: 300-500 kg (bulls), 250-350 kg (cows)
- Milk production: 1.5-3.0 liters/day
- Calving interval: 14-18 months
- Age at first calving: 36-48 months
- Dressing percentage: 48-52%

**Advantages:**
- Heat tolerant
- Tick resistant
- Hardy and adaptable
- Good for extensive grazing
- Popular for beef production

## EXOTIC/IMPROVED DAIRY BREEDS

### 9. FRIESIAN (HOLSTEIN-FRIESIAN)
**Origin:** Europe (Netherlands/Germany)
**Classification:** Bos taurus (exotic)

**Characteristics:**
- Coat color: Black and white (or red and white)
- Body type: Large, dairy frame
- No hump
- Weight: Bulls 900-1200 kg, Cows 550-700 kg

**Nutritional Requirements:**
**Growing Heifers (6-18 months):**
- CP: 14-16%
- ME: 2700-2800 kcal/kg
- TDN: 68-72%
- Ca: 0.6-0.8%
- P: 0.4-0.5%

**Lactating Cows (Peak):**
- CP: 16-18%
- ME: 2700-2850 kcal/kg
- TDN: 70-75%
- Ca: 0.8-1.0%
- P: 0.5-0.6%
- High-quality forages essential

**Dry Cows:**
- CP: 12-14%
- ME: 2500-2600 kcal/kg

**Production Data:**
- Milk production: 15-30 liters/day (Nigeria conditions)
- Milk fat: 3.5-4.0%
- Lactation length: 305 days
- Calving interval: 12-14 months
- Age at first calving: 24-28 months
- Mature weight: 550-700 kg

**Challenges in Nigeria:**
- Heat stress (requires cooling)
- Requires high-quality feed
- Disease susceptibility
- High management needs
- Expensive to maintain

**Management Requirements:**
- Air-conditioned or cooled housing
- High-quality concentrates
- Regular veterinary care
- Intensive management
- Best in controlled ranch systems

## GENERAL CATTLE NUTRITION GUIDELINES

### Feed Intake Estimates:
**Growing cattle:** 2.5-3.0% of body weight (dry matter)
**Lactating dairy:** 3.0-4.0% of body weight
**Dry cows:** 2.0-2.5% of body weight

### Water Requirements:
**Maintenance:** 25-35 liters/day
**Lactating:** 50-80 liters/day (+ 4L per liter milk)
**Hot weather:** Increase by 50-100%

### Mineral Supplementation:
Essential in Nigeria due to poor soil:
- Salt: Free choice
- Calcium & Phosphorus: Bone meal, DCP
- Trace minerals: Cu, Zn, Se, Co, I
- Vitamin A, D, E supplements

## DISEASE MANAGEMENT

### Common Cattle Diseases in Nigeria:

1. **Trypanosomiasis (Sleeping Sickness)**
   - Endemic in tsetse fly zones
   - Use trypanotolerant breeds
   - Treatment: Diminazene, Isometamidium

2. **Tick-Borne Diseases**
   - Babesiosis, Anaplasmosis, Theileriosis
   - Control: Acaricides, tick-resistant breeds

3. **Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)**
   - Highly contagious
   - Vaccination recommended

4. **Contagious Bovine Pleuropneumonia (CBPP)**
   - Respiratory disease
   - Vaccination available

5. **Helminthiasis (Worms)**
   - Common in rainy season
   - Regular deworming essential

## BREEDING & MANAGEMENT

### Breeding Systems in Nigeria:
1. **Natural Service** - Most common
2. **Artificial Insemination** - Growing
3. **Embryo Transfer** - Limited to elite herds

### Production Systems:
1. **Pastoral/Nomadic** - Traditional, extensive
2. **Semi-intensive** - Small holder, tethering
3. **Intensive/Zero-grazing** - Dairy farms, feedlots
4. **Ranch systems** - Modern, large-scale

### Recommended Practices:
- Proper identification (ear tags)
- Regular health checks
- Deworming (quarterly)
- Vaccination schedule
- Record keeping
- Hoof trimming
- Heat stress management
