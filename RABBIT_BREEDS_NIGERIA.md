## GENERAL RABBIT NUTRITION GUIDELINES FOR NIGERIA

### Feed Composition Targets:
**Starter/Grower (0-12 weeks):**
- Crude Protein: 16-18%
- Crude Fiber: 12-14%
- Energy (ME): 2700-2750 kcal/kg
- Calcium: 0.8-1.0%
- Phosphorus: 0.5-0.7%

**Maintenance (Non-breeding adults):**
- Crude Protein: 13-15%
- Crude Fiber: 14-16%
- Energy (ME): 2600-2650 kcal/kg
- Calcium: 0.6-0.8%
- Phosphorus: 0.4-0.6%

**Lactation (Nursing does):**
- Crude Protein: 17-19%
- Crude Fiber: 12-14%
- Energy (ME): 2750-2800 kcal/kg
- Calcium: 1.0-1.2%
- Phosphorus: 0.6-0.8%

### Common Nigerian Feed Ingredients:
**Forages (40-60% of diet):**
- Guinea grass (Panicum maximum)
- Elephant grass (Pennisetum purpureum)
- Pawpaw leaves
- Sweet potato vines
- Groundnut haulms
- Cowpea haulms
- Moringa leaves

**Energy Sources:**
- Maize
- Wheat offal
- Rice bran
- Cassava meal (limited to 20%)

**Protein Sources:**
- Soybean meal
- Groundnut cake
- Brewers dried grain
- Fishmeal (2-5%)

**Minerals & Supplements:**
- Bone meal
- Limestone
- Salt
- Premix

### Feed Intake Guidelines:
- Does (maintenance): 150-200 g/day
- Pregnant does: 200-250 g/day
- Lactating does: 300-450 g/day (ad libitum)
- Growing rabbits: Free choice (80-150 g/day depending on age)
- Bucks (maintenance): 150-180 g/day

### Water Requirements:
- Minimum: 250-500 ml/day
- Lactating does: Up to 1000 ml/day
- Always provide clean, fresh water

## BREEDING CONSIDERATIONS

### Breeding Age:
- Does: 6-8 months (medium breeds), 5-6 months (small breeds)
- Bucks: 6-8 months (medium breeds), 5-6 months (small breeds)

### Gestation Period:
- 28-32 days (average 31 days)

### Kindling (Birth):
- Provide nesting box 3-4 days before due date
- Does pull fur to line nest
- Kits born hairless with eyes closed

### Weaning:
- 6-8 weeks of age
- Gradual transition to solid feed starting at 3 weeks

## DISEASE MANAGEMENT

### Common Diseases in Nigeria:
1. **Coccidiosis** - Most common, especially in young rabbits
   - Prevention: Hygiene, dry cages, coccidiostats in feed
   
2. **Ear canker/Mange** - Caused by mites
   - Treatment: Ivermectin, clean housing
   
3. **Snuffles (Pasteurellosis)** - Respiratory disease
   - Prevention: Good ventilation, avoid stress
   
4. **Mastitis** - In lactating does
   - Prevention: Clean nest boxes, regular checking

### Vaccination Schedule:
- RHD (Rabbit Hemorrhagic Disease) - If available in Nigeria
- Consult local veterinarian for region-specific vaccines

## RECOMMENDED PRACTICES FOR NIGERIAN CLIMATE

1. **Housing:** 
   - Well-ventilated cages
   - Roof overhang for shade
   - Raised cages (60-80 cm) for air circulation
   - Wire mesh floor (12.5 mm gauge)

2. **Heat Management:**
   - Provide shade at all times
   - Spray water on roof during extreme heat
   - Ensure adequate ventilation
   - Fresh cool water always available

3. **Feeding Strategy:**
   - Feed in early morning and evening
   - Store feed in cool, dry place
   - Use feed within 3 months of production
   - Introduce new feeds gradually

4. **Sanitation:**
   - Remove droppings daily
   - Disinfect cages monthly
   - Quarantine new stock for 2 weeks
   - Footbath at farm entrance
